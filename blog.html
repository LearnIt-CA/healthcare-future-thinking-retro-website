Why I Think We're Wrong to Call Obesity a "Lifestyle Choice"
Posted by: Dr. Liesel Hoffmann, nutrition researcher • Personal academic blog • 2000 • Permalink • Email

Most of my colleagues still describe obesity as a problem of willpower: eat less, move more, and the weight comes off. I've spent the last few years studying weight regulation closely, and I no longer think that explanation holds up.

The body defends a set weight far more aggressively than diet culture admits, through hormones, metabolism, and genetics working together against simple willpower. Treating it purely as a lifestyle choice ignores all of that biology, and it leaves patients blaming themselves for something closer to a medical condition.

I expect this view will stay a minority position for a while yet. But I don't think it stays a minority position forever.

The Scientist Nobody Will Fund
Posted by: M. Okafor, science journalist • Personal research blog • 2000 • Permalink • Email

I spent an afternoon this week with Katalin Karikó, a Hungarian-American biochemist who has spent over a decade chasing an idea most of her field has quietly written off: that a molecule called mRNA could instruct your own cells to manufacture medicine on demand, instead of growing the drug in a lab.

It sounds elegant. It's also been almost impossible to get funded, with grant committee after grant committee turning her down. I'm not a biochemist, so I can't tell you if she's right. But she doesn't talk like someone chasing a fantasy.
