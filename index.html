Surgeon in New York Operates on Patient in France Without Leaving His Chair
The New York Times • September 9, 2001 • Carol Marin, Science Desk

In a procedure surgeons are calling the "Lindbergh Operation," a team led by Dr. Jacques Marescaux removed a gallbladder from a patient in Strasbourg, France, while Dr. Marescaux sat at a robotic control console in Manhattan. The operation took 54 minutes. The patient was discharged two days later with no complications.

Not everyone shares the enthusiasm. "One successful procedure over a purpose-built private cable is a long way from a clinical reality," said Dr. Patricia Wells, chair of surgery at Columbia University Medical Center.
